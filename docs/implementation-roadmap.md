# DORA Implementation Roadmap

A suggested order for building a DORA programme from scratch. It groups the work into four phases. The weeks are a guide, not a rule. A large firm will take longer; a small firm using the simplified framework will do less. Do the phases in order, because later work depends on earlier work.

**Read first:** [Glossary](glossary.md) and [Proportionality guide](proportionality-guide.md).

---

## Phase 1: Set the foundation (roughly weeks 1 to 8)

You cannot manage what you have not defined. This phase is about ownership and knowing your estate.

- Confirm scope and proportionality. Write down whether you use the full or simplified framework, and why. (See the proportionality guide.)
- Name the accountable board member or committee for ICT risk.
- Define the operating model: three lines of defence and a RACI.
- Start the asset and dependency inventory. List your top business functions and mark which are critical or important.
- Draft the resilience strategy with risk appetite.

**End of phase check:** the board owns ICT risk, you know your proportionality tier, and you have a first inventory of critical functions.

## Phase 2: Build the core controls (roughly weeks 6 to 20)

Now protect what you identified, and be able to spot and survive trouble.

- Approve the ICT risk framework at board level.
- Write the information security policy and control standards.
- Set up detection: logging and alerting on critical systems.
- Define RTO and RPO for critical functions, write recovery plans, and set up segregated backups.
- Stand up the incident process, the classification aid (with RTS thresholds), and the reporting runbook (with ITS deadlines and a named owner).

**End of phase check:** you can protect, detect, recover, and you have a working, owned incident-reporting runbook.

## Phase 3: Test and manage the supply chain (roughly weeks 16 to 32)

Prove the controls work, and get your third parties under control.

- Build the risk-based testing programme and test calendar. Test all critical systems.
- Set up the findings workflow so issues get fixed and re-tested.
- Check with your regulator whether you are named for TLPT. If so, start the TLPT cycle.
- Build the register of information and reconcile it to real spend and cloud usage.
- Review contracts for required and enhanced terms. Fix audit rights and exit terms first.
- Write and test exit strategies for critical functions.

**End of phase check:** critical systems are tested, findings are fixed, the register is complete, and critical-function contracts and exit plans are sound.

## Phase 4: Measure, report, and improve (ongoing)

Turn a project into a living programme.

- Set up board reporting with clear metrics and legal-breach flags.
- Run a timed incident exercise against the real reporting deadline.
- Do post-incident reviews and feed lessons back into controls.
- Decide on information sharing and, if you join, notify the regulator.
- Run the yearly framework review, the yearly register submission, and internal audit over ICT.

**End of phase check:** the programme measures itself, reports honestly to the board, and improves each cycle.

---

## Quick priority guide if you are behind

If you have limited time and need to reduce the biggest risks first, do these in this order:

1. Make sure you can report a major incident on time. This is a hard legal duty with a tight clock.
2. Prove you can restore a critical system from a segregated backup.
3. Complete the register of information and fix missing audit and exit terms for your most important providers.
4. Test all critical systems and fix the serious findings.
5. Get the board to formally own and approve the framework.

These five cover the areas where failure hurts most, either legally or operationally.

## How this maps to the pillars

| Phase | Main pillars involved |
|-------|-----------------------|
| 1 Foundation | Governance (0), start of ICT risk (1) |
| 2 Core controls | ICT risk (1), Incident management (2) |
| 3 Test and supply chain | Testing (3), Third-party risk (4) |
| 4 Measure and improve | All, plus Information sharing (5) |
