# Pillar 5 — Information & Intelligence Sharing

**DORA article:** Art. 45 (information-sharing arrangements on cyber threat information and intelligence).

---

## 1. Purpose

DORA encourages, but does not mandate, financial entities to share **cyber threat information and intelligence** among themselves to enhance collective digital operational resilience. This pillar is voluntary but strategically valuable: threat intelligence sharing improves early detection and response across the sector.

## 2. Key DORA requirements (Art. 45)

Financial entities **may** exchange amongst themselves cyber threat information and intelligence, including indicators of compromise, tactics, techniques and procedures (TTPs), cyber security alerts and configuration tools, where such sharing:
- aims to enhance digital operational resilience, in particular through raising awareness, containing the spread of threats, supporting defensive capabilities, threat detection techniques, mitigation strategies, or response and recovery stages;
- takes place within **trusted communities** of financial entities;
- is implemented through **information-sharing arrangements** that protect the potentially sensitive nature of the information and are governed by rules of conduct fully respecting business confidentiality, protection of personal data (in line with the GDPR), and competition-policy guidelines.

Financial entities must **notify competent authorities** of their participation in such information-sharing arrangements upon validation of their membership, or of the cessation of membership once it takes effect.

## 3. Control objectives

| ID | Control objective |
|----|-------------------|
| ISH-01 | A decision on participation in threat-intelligence sharing is documented and risk-based. |
| ISH-02 | Sharing occurs within trusted communities under formal arrangements. |
| ISH-03 | Arrangements protect confidentiality, personal data (GDPR) and comply with competition rules. |
| ISH-04 | The competent authority is notified of joining or leaving an arrangement. |
| ISH-05 | Shared intelligence is operationally integrated into detection and response. |

## 4. Implementation steps

1. **Assess value & risk** of participating in sector information-sharing communities (e.g. sector CERTs, ISACs).
2. **Select trusted communities** and review their governance, membership vetting and handling rules (e.g. traffic-light protocol).
3. **Establish the arrangement** with data-protection, confidentiality and competition safeguards; obtain legal review.
4. **Notify the competent authority** upon validated membership and upon cessation.
5. **Integrate intake**: route received indicators/TTPs into detection tooling and threat-intel processes; contribute back where appropriate.
6. **Govern ongoing participation**: periodic review of value, risk and compliance.

## 5. Evidence to retain

- Participation decision and risk assessment.
- Signed information-sharing arrangement and governance/handling rules.
- Legal/DPO review of confidentiality, GDPR and competition considerations.
- Notifications to the competent authority (join/leave).
- Records showing shared intelligence is operationally used.

## 6. Common pitfalls

- Sharing outside a formal, trusted arrangement, creating confidentiality or competition-law exposure.
- Receiving intelligence but not operationalising it (no integration with detection/response).
- Forgetting to notify the competent authority of membership changes.
