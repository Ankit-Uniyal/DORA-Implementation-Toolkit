# Model Policies (Skeleton Drafts)

**These are starting drafts, not finished policies, and not legal advice.** DORA
does not hand you a set of policies. It requires you to *have* a policy framework
that meets the articles, owned and approved by your management body. This folder
gives a first-timer a running start: skeleton policies for the core documents a
DORA programme is expected to hold, written in the same plain-English style as the
rest of the toolkit.

Replace every bracketed placeholder with your real facts, delete what does not
apply, and have the right owner and the board approve the final version. A model
policy you paste in unchanged is evidence of nothing.

> Keep every regulatory figure (thresholds, deadlines) pointing to
> > [docs/regulatory-references.md](../../docs/regulatory-references.md). Do not
> > > hard-code a number in a policy that could drift from the law.

> > >
> > > ## How to use them
> > >
> > > 1. Read the matching implementation playbook in `docs/2-implementation/` first, so
> > > 2.    you understand what the policy has to make happen.
> > > 3.2. Fill the placeholders with your real roles, systems, and numbers.
> > >   3. Cross-check the policy against the relevant control objectives (GOV/RM/INC/TST/TPP)
> > >   4.    and the [self-assessment questionnaire](../../docs/1-gap-assessment/self-assessment-questionnaire.md).
> > >   5.4. Route it through the approval path in your governance model (usually: draft by
> > >        the owner, review by risk/legal, approval by the board or its risk committee).
> > >     5. Version it, date it, and set a yearly review. Record the approval in board
> > >        minutes (that record is itself evidence for GOV-01).
> > >
> > > ## Common mistakes
> > >
> > > - Adopting a model policy word-for-word without tailoring it. Assessors spot this
> > > -   instantly, and it will not match how you actually operate.
> > > -   - Writing a policy that promises controls you do not run. A policy you breach is
> > >     -   worse than a gap you have flagged honestly.
> > >     -   - Hard-coding legal figures into the policy instead of referencing the single
> > >         -   source of truth, so the policy silently goes stale when the law changes.
