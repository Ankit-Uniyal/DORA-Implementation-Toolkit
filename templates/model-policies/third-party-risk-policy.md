## 1. Purpose

Set out how [Entity] manages the risk from ICT third-party service providers, especially those supporting critical or important functions. [Entity] can outsource the activity but not the responsibility (Art. 28).

## 2. Life-cycle approach

**Before contracting.** Assess criticality, do due diligence (financial and operational health, security posture, data locations, sub-outsourcing, concentration, conflicts), and confirm the required and enhanced contract terms are achievable.

**Contracting.** Every ICT contract holds the base terms; contracts for critical or important functions hold the enhanced terms (audit/access/inspection rights, security requirements, TLPT cooperation, incident support, sub-outsourcing transparency, termination and exit assistance, regulator cooperation) per Art. 30. Notify the competent authority in good time of planned arrangements supporting a critical or important function (Art. 28(3)).

**During the relationship.** Monitor service levels, incidents, sub-outsourcing changes, concentration, and CTPP status. Maintain the register of information and report it to the competent authority at least yearly.

**Exit.** For each critical or important function, hold a written, tested exit strategy.

## 3. Register of information

[Entity] keeps the register per the Register ITS (EU) 2024/2956, reconciled to accounts-payable and cloud/SaaS inventories for completeness, marking which arrangements support critical or important functions. See the [third-party register template](../third-party-register.md).

## 4. Where leverage is limited (small firms vs hyperscalers)

Where [Entity] cannot redline a large provider's terms, it will: attach the provider's published DORA addendum; accept proportionate audit substitutes (pooled audits, SOC 2 Type II, ISO/IEC 27001, ISAE 3402) with the reason recorded; reduce lock-in technically (portable formats, independent backups); treat concentration honestly as a board-approved, monitored residual risk; and escalate any genuinely unobtainable required term to the management body for a documented accept/mitigate/exit decision. See playbook 04, section 14.

## 5. Concentration and sub-outsourcing

[Entity] assesses concentration (many critical functions on one provider or group) and maps sub-outsourcing chains, including third-country exposure, per the Subcontracting RTS (EU) 2025/532. See the [sub-outsourcing and concentration method](../../docs/2-implementation/sub-outsourcing-and-concentration.md).

## 6. Roles

[Head of Third-Party Risk] owns the policy and register; Procurement runs due diligence; Legal reviews contracts; the CISO assesses security; business owners own exit plans; the management body approves the strategy and residual-risk decisions.

## 8. Review

Reviewed at least yearly; re-approved by the management body (evidence for TPP-01 to TPP-09).
